Sepsis Appeal Engine

AI-Assisted Appeal Letter Generation

What It Does

When we receive a denial letter for a sepsis case, this system helps draft an appeal letter by automatically pulling together all the relevant information and generating a first draft.

You still review and approve every letter. The AI does the research and drafting; you provide the judgment and final approval.

The Simple Flow

Denial Letter (PDF) | v +------------------+ | AI System | | | | Pulls together: | | - Past winners | | - Clinical notes| | - Lab values | | - Vitals | | - Medications | | - Diagnoses | +------------------+ | v Draft Appeal Letter (Word doc) | v CDI Review & Approval | v Final Letter to Payor

What Goes In

1. The Denial Letter

The PDF denial letter from the payor. The system reads this to understand what arguments we need to counter.

2. Past Winning Appeals (Gold Letters)

A library of our past successful appeals. The system finds the most similar past case and uses that winning letter as a template - proven arguments get reused.

3. Clinical Notes from Epic

The system pulls 14 types of clinical notes for this patient:

  • Progress Notes, H&P, Discharge Summary
  • ED Notes, ED Triage, ED Provider Notes
  • Consults, Nursing Notes
  • And more...

4. Structured Data from Epic

Objective clinical measurements:

  • Labs: Lactate, WBC, procalcitonin, cultures, creatinine, bilirubin, platelets
  • Vitals: Temperature, MAP, heart rate, respiratory rate, SpO2, GCS
  • Medications: Antibiotics, vasopressors, fluids
  • Diagnoses: Documented diagnosis descriptions (e.g., "Severe sepsis with septic shock due to MRSA")

5. Propel Criteria

The official clinical definition of sepsis that we use to justify the diagnosis.

What Comes Out

A Word Document with:

  • Strength Assessment - A score (1-10) rating how strong the appeal is, with notes on what evidence is present vs. missing
  • The Appeal Letter - A complete draft addressing the denial arguments with clinical evidence
  • Conflicts Flag - If the physician notes say one thing but the labs/vitals show something different, this is flagged for your review

How It Builds the Letter

1

Read the Denial

Extracts the payor's arguments and identifies what we need to counter.

2

Find a Similar Past Winner

Searches our library of past successful appeals to find the most similar case. Uses that winning letter as a template.

3

Gather Clinical Evidence

Pulls all relevant notes, labs, vitals, meds, and diagnoses from Epic for this patient.

4

Check for Conflicts

Compares what the physician notes say vs. what the structured data shows. Flags any discrepancies for your review.

5

Generate the Appeal

Writes the letter using the winning template, clinical evidence, and Propel criteria to build the argument.

6

Assess Strength

Evaluates the letter and flags what evidence is strong vs. what might be missing.

Evidence Hierarchy

The system treats different types of evidence differently:

Evidence Type Role Example
Physician Notes Primary evidence - clinical judgment "Patient developed septic shock requiring vasopressors"
Structured Data Supporting evidence - objective measurements MAP 58, Lactate 4.2, Norepinephrine started 14:30
Diagnoses Documented conditions to quote "Severe sepsis with septic shock due to E. coli"

What You Need to Review

Every Letter Needs Your Approval

The AI generates a draft, but you:

  • Verify the clinical facts are accurate
  • Check that the arguments make sense for this case
  • Review any flagged conflicts
  • Edit as needed before sending

Key Benefits

  • Faster drafting - Research and initial draft done automatically
  • Consistent quality - Uses proven arguments from past winners
  • Complete evidence - Pulls from 14 note types plus labs, vitals, meds
  • Conflict detection - Flags discrepancies between notes and data
  • You stay in control - Every letter reviewed before sending
